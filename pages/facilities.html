Information About Screening Facilities

Types of Facilities

Breast cancer screening may be available at various locations including hospitals, clinics, imaging centers, and mobile screening units. Each offers different services and levels of care.

Financial Assistance

Many facilities offer financial assistance programs or sliding scale fees. Some accept insurance, Medicaid, or Medicare. The CDC's National Breast and Cervical Cancer Early Detection Program (NBCCEDP) provides free or low-cost screenings.

What to Expect

Most screening appointments take 30 minutes or less. You'll be asked to remove clothing from the waist up and wear a gown. The technologist will position each breast for the mammogram, which may cause brief discomfort.

Preparing for Your Visit

Schedule your mammogram when your breasts are least tender. Don't wear deodorant, talcum powder, or lotion under your arms or on your breasts on the day of the exam. Bring your insurance card and any previous mammogram records.
