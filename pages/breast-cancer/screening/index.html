Screening and Early Detection

Regular screening tests are the most reliable way to find breast cancer early. Learn about different screening methods, when to get screened, and what to do if you find changes in your breasts.

Screening Recommendations

For Women at Average Risk

Ages 40-74

  • Have a mammogram every 1-2 years
  • Clinical breast exams every 1-3 years
  • Be familiar with how your breasts normally look and feel

Ages 75 and Older

  • Discuss with your healthcare provider
  • Consider your overall health and life expectancy
  • Continue screening as long as you're in good health

For Women at Higher Risk

  • Start screening at a younger age
  • Get screened more often
  • Use additional screening tests like MRI
  • Consider genetic counseling and testing

Note: Talk to your healthcare provider about your personal risk factors and the screening plan that's right for you.

Mammography

Types of Mammograms

Screening Mammogram

Regular check for breast cancer in women who have no signs or symptoms.

Diagnostic Mammogram

Used to check for breast cancer after a lump or other sign or symptom has been found.

3D Mammogram

Creates a three-dimensional picture of the breast using X-rays.

What to Expect During a Mammogram

  1. You'll need to undress from the waist up
  2. A technologist will position your breast on a special platform
  3. Your breast will be compressed between two plates
  4. The technologist will take multiple images from different angles
  5. The entire process takes about 20-30 minutes

Tips for Your Mammogram

  • Don't wear deodorant, perfume, or powder on the day of your exam
  • Schedule your mammogram when your breasts are least tender
  • Wear a two-piece outfit for easy undressing
  • Bring your previous mammogram images if you're going to a new facility

Understanding Your Results

Normal Result

No signs of breast cancer found. Continue regular screening as recommended.

Need Additional Imaging

Additional views or other tests needed to make a complete evaluation.

Suspicious Finding

An area that needs to be checked with a biopsy.

Clinical Breast Exam

A clinical breast exam is a physical exam done by a healthcare provider to check for breast problems or abnormalities.

What to Expect

  • Visual inspection of your breasts
  • Physical examination of your breast tissue
  • Check for changes in size, shape, or texture
  • Examination of your underarm area

Breast Self-Awareness

Being breast aware means knowing how your breasts normally look and feel and reporting any changes to your healthcare provider.

Know Your Normal

  • Size and shape
  • Skin texture
  • The look and feel of your nipples
  • Any usual tenderness or pain

Report These Changes

  • A lump or thickening in or near the breast or underarm
  • Changes in breast size or shape
  • Dimpling or puckering of the skin
  • Nipple discharge (other than breast milk)
  • Nipple inversion or other nipple changes
