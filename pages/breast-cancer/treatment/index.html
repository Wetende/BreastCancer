Treatment Planning

Your treatment plan will be based on many factors, including:

  • Stage and grade of the cancer
  • Hormone receptor status
  • HER2 status
  • Your age and overall health
  • Your preferences

Your Treatment Team

Breast Surgeon

Performs breast cancer surgery and biopsies.

Medical Oncologist

Manages chemotherapy, hormone therapy, and other drug treatments.

Radiation Oncologist

Plans and delivers radiation therapy.

Surgery

Lumpectomy (Breast-Conserving Surgery)

Removes the tumor and a small margin of healthy tissue around it.

Benefits

  • Preserves most of the breast
  • Shorter recovery time
  • Usually done as outpatient surgery

Mastectomy

Removes the entire breast.

Types of Mastectomy

  • Total (simple) mastectomy
  • Modified radical mastectomy
  • Skin-sparing mastectomy
  • Nipple-sparing mastectomy

Lymph Node Surgery

Checks if cancer has spread to lymph nodes.

Types

  • Sentinel node biopsy
  • Axillary node dissection

Radiation Therapy

Uses high-energy rays to kill cancer cells.

External Beam Radiation

Most common type, delivered from outside the body.

Internal Radiation (Brachytherapy)

Radiation source placed inside the breast.

Treatment Schedule

  • Usually 5 days a week for 3-6 weeks
  • Each session takes about 15-30 minutes
  • Shorter schedules may be options for some women

Chemotherapy

Uses drugs to kill cancer cells throughout the body.

Neoadjuvant Chemotherapy

Given before surgery to shrink tumors.

Adjuvant Chemotherapy

Given after surgery to kill any remaining cancer cells.

Common Side Effects

  • Hair loss
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Fatigue
  • Increased risk of infection

Hormone Therapy

Used for hormone receptor-positive breast cancers.

Tamoxifen

Blocks estrogen from binding to cancer cells.

Aromatase Inhibitors

Lowers estrogen levels in postmenopausal women.

Targeted Therapy

Targets specific characteristics of cancer cells.

HER2-Targeted Drugs

For HER2-positive breast cancers.

CDK4/6 Inhibitors

Help hormone therapy work better.

Clinical Trials

Clinical trials are research studies that test new treatments.

Benefits of Participating

  • Access to new treatments
  • Close monitoring by research team
  • Contribution to medical research

Our team can help you find clinical trials that might be right for you.

Managing Side Effects

Side effects vary depending on the type of treatment.

Physical Side Effects

  • Fatigue
  • Pain
  • Nausea
  • Lymphedema

Emotional Side Effects

  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Body image concerns
  • Fear of recurrence

Support Services

We offer various support services to help you manage side effects:

  • Pain management
  • Lymphedema therapy
  • Counseling
  • Support groups

Getting Quality Care

Quality Care Checklist

  • Choose an experienced healthcare team
  • Seek care at accredited centers
  • Ask questions about your treatment
  • Keep detailed records
  • Bring a support person to appointments
